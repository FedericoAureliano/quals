Message Chains v. k-States
  • Verification engines often offer something like an old keyword
    • e.g., Boogie and mypyvy
  • This gives users access to previous states
    • A k-state predicate is one that looks k-1 states back
    • But usually only one step back is allowed (old is idempotent)
    • So old is like an extremely high-definition, wide-angle picture of what just happened
  • Message chains can hold information from any number of previous states
    • But less information is kept from each state
      • So message chains are more like a movie
      • Our instrumentation defines where the filmmaker is standing
Leino (2008)
