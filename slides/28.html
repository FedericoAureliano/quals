Unjustified Constraint Example
  • Input strings:
    • abcd
    • cbd
    • dc
  • Output pattern: XY
  • Unjustified constraint (BAD): |X| = 1
    • e.g., the assignment {X ↦ abc, Y ↦ d} can justify abcd ∈ L(XY)
  • Note that L(XY) = L(XY ∧ |X| = 1)
    • We are adding semantic constraints to a syntactic notion
