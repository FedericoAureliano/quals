Non-Erasing Pattern Learning, Generally
  • Non-Erasing Patterns
    • Let Σ and V be two disjoint sets of symbols with at least two elements each
    • A non-erasing pattern is a finite sequence of symbols drawn from Σ and V
    • A string is a finite sequence of symbols drawn from Σ
    • Every non-erasing pattern p represents a language, L(p). For a given pattern p and string s
      • If there is a mapping μ from V to Σ+ such that μ(p) = s, then s ∈ L(p)
      • Otherwise, s ∉ L(p)
  • The Learning Problem:
    • Input: set of strings S
    • Output: a non-erasing pattern p such that
      • L(p) ⊆ S
      • p is the most specific pattern
Angluin (1980)
