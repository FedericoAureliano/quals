Invariants, Generally
  • The state of the system is the
    • state of every node, and
    • the state of the message buffer.
  • An invariant is a predicate over the state of the system.
  • Invariants can encode
    • safety properties, and
    • some types of liveness properties.
  • A system transition is a pair of states (s, s'), where s' is the result of a node's action on s.
  • An invariant I is inductive if I(s) ⇒ I(s'), where (s, s') is a transition.
Fang, McMillan, Pnueli, and Zuck (2006); Lynch (1996)
